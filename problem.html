Shocking are the facts and figures on the impact of toxic air quality. In a special report on world energy research, the International Energy Agency reports that approximately 18,000 people die every day as a result of air pollution. This is equivalent to 6.5 million deaths worldwide each year, much more than HIV / AIDS, tuberculosis and road injuries combined! Air pollution is the fourth largest global risk factor for human health worldwide, after high blood pressure, dietary hazards and smoking.

As the Agency points out, the vast amount of air pollution is caused by electricity production. Dirty energy is destroying our climate in many respects as well. Although all energy sources somehow affect our climate, fossil / dirty energy causes more harm than renewables.
